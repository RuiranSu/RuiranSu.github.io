  • Automatic scoring of secondary school subjective questions using knowledge graph

    • The automatic assessment work for subjective questions in Chinese (including history, geography, politics, etc.) mainly serves the needs of small-scale teacher-assisted marking, with pre-training corpus crawled from subject textbooks, subject websites, tutorial materials, etc. In addition to this, real test paper data is indispensable, and thanks to our college's good relationships with government and business enterprises, we have attained access to some provincial and municipal Chinese examination data. The process of converting unstructured texts into structured ones, the decryption of e-books, the anti-crawl mechanism of crawler websites and OCR proofreading are all problems that must be solved. Reference answers can be obtained and parsed through entity extraction and entity relationship extraction and the construction of a knowledge graph, and candidate answers can be compared with reference answers through semantic consistency analysis and sentiment disposition analysis.

  • Text analysis and sentiment analysis of China-related stories in the New York Times and Los Angeles Post, 2010-present

    • In recent years, with China's economy taking off, foreign media coverage of China's economy, society and politics has been soaring. The image of China presented by the US mainstream media in their reports not only reflects to a certain extent the evaluation of China by Western public opinion, but also serves as a significant microcosm of China's international image. The three major mainstream publications in the US, namely the New York Times, the Los Angeles Post and the Washington Post, have published a total of over 30,000 China-related articles since 2010. By means of entity extraction, attribute extraction and sentiment classification, the analysis can be divided into three dimensions. To be specific, the macro framework includes the number of reports, subject distribution, subject weighting, and overall trends. The meso-framework includes the location of the article and the source of the information, etc. The micro framework includes high frequency words and key sentences, comparison of tendency words, etc.

  • Introducing automatic 5G network slicing on the Open Network Automation Platform

    •   

    Intent-based network (IBN) is a self-driving network that uses decoupling network control logic and closed-loop orchestration techniques to automate application intents. An IBN is an intelligent network, which can automatically convert, verify, deploy, configure, and optimize itself to achieve target network state according to the intent of the operators, and can automatically solve abnormal events to ensure the network reliability. End-to-end slicing provides more opportunities for telecommunications in vertical industry. It is able to satisfy the different Service Level Agreement of vertical industry. However, a smarter and more flexible slicing management framework is required to support the deep application of vertical industry in beyond 5G networks. The slicing research and standardization of 3GPP and other SDOs has started to focus on smart slicing. A significant target of smart slicing is to satisfy the users’ intents and requirements. An intent-based network can recognize the users’ intent in real-time and then update the network slicing to guarantee the SLA.
